Classic Meatloaf Recipe

Description

Classic Meatloaf is a beloved American comfort food that has graced dinner tables for generations. This hearty dish features seasoned ground beef mixed with breadcrumbs, eggs, and savory seasonings, all topped with a tangy ketchup glaze. It's the ultimate family-friendly meal that delivers both flavor and nostalgia.

This traditional recipe is simple to prepare and yields a moist, flavorful meatloaf that pairs perfectly with mashed potatoes and green beans. The secret to a great meatloaf is not over mixing the meat and letting it rest after baking. Leftovers make amazing sandwiches the next day!

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds ground beef
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 cup ketchup (for topping)
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar (for topping)

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the ground beef, breadcrumbs, chopped onion, beaten eggs, milk, Worcestershire sauce, salt, pepper, and garlic powder.
  3. Mix the ingredients gently with your hands until just combined. Do not over mix.
  4. Shape the mixture into a loaf and place it in a greased 9x5 inch loaf pan or on a baking sheet.
  5. In a small bowl, mix together the ketchup and brown sugar to make the glaze.
  6. Spread half of the glaze over the top of the meatloaf.
  7. Bake for 45 minutes, then spread the remaining glaze on top.
  8. Continue baking for another 15-20 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 160 degrees Fahrenheit.
  9. Remove from oven and let rest for 10 minutes before slicing.
  10. Slice and serve hot with your favorite side dishes.
