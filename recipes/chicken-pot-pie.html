Chicken Pot Pie Recipe

Description

Chicken Pot Pie is a classic American comfort food that combines tender chicken, mixed vegetables, and a creamy sauce, all baked under a golden, flaky crust. This hearty dish is perfect for cold evenings and family dinners, offering warmth and satisfaction in every bite.

This home style recipe brings together simple ingredients to create a meal that feels like a warm hug. The combination of savory filling and buttery crust makes this dish a favorite across generations. While it takes some time to prepare, the result is a delicious, crowd-pleasing dinner that's worth every minute.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked chicken, diced
  • 1 cup carrots, diced
  • 1 cup peas
  • 1 cup celery, diced
  • 1/3 cup butter
  • 1/3 cup flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 2/3 cup milk
  • 2 pie crusts

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 425 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. In a large pot, melt the butter over medium heat.
  3. Add the flour, salt, and pepper, stirring constantly to create a paste. Cook for one minute.
  4. Slowly add the chicken broth and milk, stirring constantly until the mixture thickens and becomes smooth.
  5. Add the diced chicken, carrots, peas, and celery to the sauce. Stir well and remove from heat.
  6. Place one pie crust in a 9-inch pie pan.
  7. Pour the chicken and vegetable mixture into the bottom crust.
  8. Cover with the second pie crust, seal the edges, and cut small slits in the top to allow steam to escape.
  9. Bake for 30 to 35 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.
  10. Let cool for 10 minutes before serving.
